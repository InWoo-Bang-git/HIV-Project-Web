Why test for HIV?

HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) treatment involves the use of antiretroviral therapy (ART) to manage the virus and maintain a healthy immune system. The goals of HIV treatment are to:

  • Suppress the virus to undetectable levels in the blood.
  • Restore and preserve the immune system's function.
  • Prevent HIV-related complications and progression to AIDS.
  • Reduce the risk of transmitting HIV to others.

ART typically consists of a combination of different antiretroviral drugs, which work by interfering with various stages of the HIV life cycle. There are several classes of these drugs, including nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NRTIs), non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NNRTIs), protease inhibitors (PIs), integrase strand transfer inhibitors (INSTIs), and entry inhibitors.

Common antiretroviral drugs

1.NRTIs (Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors):

  • Common drugs: Tenofovir, Emtricitabine, Zidovudine, Lamivudine.
  • Side effects: Nausea, diarrhea, neuropathy, bone marrow suppression, lactic acidosis (rare).

2.NNRTIs (Non-Nucleoside Reverse Transcriptase Inhibitors):

  • Common drugs: Efavirenz, Nevirapine, Rilpivirine.
  • Side effects: Rash, liver toxicity, mood changes, central nervous system symptoms (e.g., vivid dreams, dizziness).

3. PIs (Protease Inhibitors):

  • Common drugs: Atazanavir, Darunavir, Lopinavir.
  • Side effects: Gastrointestinal issues (nausea, diarrhea), metabolic disturbances (lipodystrophy, elevated cholesterol/triglycerides), potential drug interactions.
